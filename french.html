Micro Grammar Notes (must memorize)

  • Word order: French basic SVO (Subject-Verb-Object): Je mange une pomme.
  • Negation: ne + verb + pas. Spoken: often verb + pas.
  • Questions: 3 types — intonation (tu viens ?), est-ce que (Est-ce que tu viens ?), inversion (Viens-tu ?). Use intonation for beginners.
  • Linking: Use liaison when next word begins with vowel: les amis — pronounce the s as /z/: lay-zah-mee.

4. Basic Sentence Structure

In French, the standard sentence order is the same as English: Subject + Verb + Object.

Example patterns:

  • Je parle français. (zhuh parl frahn-say) → I speak French.
  • Tu manges une pomme. (too mahnzh oon pohm) → You eat an apple.
  • Elle aime la musique. (ell em lah myoo-zeek) → She likes music.

Note:

French verbs change endings depending on the subject — this is called conjugation. The patterns you learn with “parler” (to speak) will apply to most verbs ending in “-er”.

3. Building Questions in French

There are three main ways to form questions:

  1. Intonation — simply raise your voice at the end of a statement.
    Tu viens ? → (too vyahn?) → “You’re coming?”
  2. “Est-ce que” + statement — polite and common.
    Est-ce que tu parles anglais ? → (ess kuh too parl ahn-glay?) → Do you speak English?
  3. Inversion — formal or written French.
    Parles-tu anglais ? → (parl too ahn-glay?) → Do you speak English?

  • J’ai peur. — (zhay puhr) → I’m afraid.

3. Opinions and Preferences

Learning how to express what you *like*, *dislike*, or *prefer* builds your conversational depth.

  • J’aime... — (zhem) → I like...
  • Je n’aime pas... — (zhuh nem pah) → I don’t like...
  • J’adore... — (zhah-dor) → I love...
  • Je préfère... — (zhuh pray-fair) → I prefer...
  • Je déteste... — (zhuh day-test) → I hate...

Examples:

J’aime le café. — I like coffee.
Je préfère le thé. — I prefer tea.
Je déteste le bruit. — I hate noise.
J’adore la musique française. — I love French music.

5. Social Connection & Politeness Phrases

Politeness is crucial in French culture — it conveys respect and emotional intelligence.

  • Enchanté(e). — (ahn-shahn-tay) → Nice to meet you.
  • Comment allez-vous ? — (koh-mahn tah-lay voo) → How are you? (formal)
  • Ça va ? — (sah vah) → How’s it going? (informal)
  • Ça va bien, merci. Et vous ? — (sah vah byan, mehr-see. ay voo?) → I’m fine, thanks. And you?
  • Bonne journée ! — (bun zhoor-nay) → Have a good day!
  • Bonne soirée ! — (bun swah-ray) → Have a good evening!
  • À bientôt ! — (ah byan-toh) → See you soon!
  • À demain ! — (ah duh-manh) → See you tomorrow!
